Getting Started

1

Installation

Add NewTabFolders for newtab to Chrome by visiting the Chrome Web Store and clicking Add to Chrome. Once installed, NewTabFolders will replace your new tab page.

💡

Pro Tip: After installation, we recommend pinning NewTabFolders's icon to your browser's toolbar for easy access.

2

Opening NewTabFolders

To launch NewTabFolders, simply open a new tab in Chrome. You should see the NewTabFolders interface instead of the usual new tab screen.

3

Quick Tour of the Interface

On the left side, you will see an Open Tabs panel listing all the tabs currently open in your browser window. On the right side, you'll find your Folders area, where you can organize bookmarks into folders. At the top, there's a Search bar that lets you find tabs quickly.

Saving and Managing Tabs

1

Drag and Drop to Save

Whenever NewTabFolders is open, your currently open sites are listed in the left sidebar. To save one, just drag its title from the Open Tabs list and drop it into a folder on the right.

2

One-Click "Stash All"

To save a whole session of many open tabs, click the 📥 (stash) button at the top of the Open Tabs panel. This is great for when you're finishing a research session.

Organizing with Folders and Colors

1

Creating and Naming Folders

You can create a new folder by clicking the "Add folder" card in the main grid or dragging a tab into a blank area.

2

Color Coding

Assign colors to your folders to categorize them. For instance, use blue for work, green for personal. To change color, click the folder menu and select a color picker option.

Using Sticky Notes

1

Creating a Note

Right-click inside a folder or on an empty area and select "Add Sticky Note". A blank note will appear, ready for your thoughts.

💡

Pro Tip: Sticky Notes are perfect for tracking to-dos or reminders related to your saved tabs.

Search and Smart Filtering

The search bar is located at the top. Type keywords to instantly filter through open tabs, saved bookmarks, and recently closed tabs.

Performance and Bulk Actions

1

Multi-Select with SHIFT

Hold SHIFT and click on items to select multiple tabs or bookmarks. You can then drag them all together to a different folder.

Importing and Exporting

In the Settings menu, you can find options to export your data as a JSON file or import bookmarks from other extensions.
